Executive Summary

The stakes are real.
So is the opportunity.

Copper State Credit Union is exploring the development of a new digital application — one positioned to win back a generation that credit unions have been losing for a decade. 61% of Gen Z already use neobanks like Chime and Cash App as their primary financial institutions. Only 11% use a credit union.

Arizona is one of the best markets in the country for this bet. With over 600,000 underbanked Arizonans and a fast-growing Gen Z population representing 26% of new state residents, the opportunity is validated and the timing is urgent. The first-mover window for a fintech-grade Gen Z brand — built on Copper State's NCUA charter — is open, but it will not stay open indefinitely.

Before committing significant capital to development, it is critical to ensure that the product strategy, user experience, and feature set are clearly defined and aligned across stakeholders. This engagement is designed to deliver exactly that — a fully interactive, clickable prototype of Copper, enabling your team to validate the experience, align leadership, and move into a $2.5M development commitment with clarity and confidence.

The prototype costs less than 2% of the full build investment. It answers the hardest question before you spend a dollar building: does this product feel right?

Scope of Work

Three phases.
No surprises.

Each phase builds on the last. Two formal review checkpoints ensure Copper State approves the direction before work advances — so the final prototype reflects your vision, not ours.

1
Product Strategy & Alignment
Weeks 1–3

Before a single screen is designed, we need to understand what Copper State needs from Copper — and what Copper's future members need from their bank. This phase turns the strategic research already completed into a validated product foundation.

Activities

  • Stakeholder interviews across leadership and key departments
  • Identification of core member journeys and use cases
  • Feature prioritization aligned to business goals and CU charter advantages
  • Strategic positioning relative to Chime, Varo, and traditional CU competitors
  • Review and refinement of existing market research (Arizona Gen Z data, 600K+ underbanked opportunity)

Deliverables

  • Product vision summary
  • Prioritized feature roadmap (launch vs. Phase 2 vs. future)
  • Defined user journeys with persona detail
  • Competitive positioning map
2
UX Design & User Flows
Weeks 3–6

With the strategy locked, we map every experience a Copper member will have — from opening an account in 90 seconds to receiving their first paycheck two days early. Structure and logic come before visual polish. This phase ends with your approval of the full wireframe set.

Activities

  • End-to-end user flow mapping across 5 core experiences
  • Wireframing all 30 application screens
  • Defining interaction logic, navigation structure, and edge states
  • Wireframe review checkpoint with Copper State leadership
  • Revisions and sign-off before advancing to visual design

Deliverables

  • User flow diagrams (5 complete journeys)
  • 30 low- to mid-fidelity wireframes
  • Experience architecture document
  • Checkpoint review presentation
